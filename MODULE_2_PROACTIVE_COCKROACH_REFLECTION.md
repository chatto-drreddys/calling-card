# Module 2 Reflection: Be Proactive and the Cockroach Story

## Reflection Prompt

Give one "cockroach" that you have overcome by being proactive.

## Personal Reflection

One personal "cockroach" I have overcome by being proactive is allowing work stress to spill into my family life.

As a married man and a father to my daughter, I realized that pressure from work could quietly affect how I showed up at home. There were times when mental exhaustion made me less patient, less present, and more emotionally distant. My family was not the cause of that stress, but they could still feel its impact.

I learned that the real problem was not only the workload. The real problem was my response to it. Instead of reacting through frustration, silence, or irritability, I chose to become more proactive. I started setting clearer boundaries between work and home, planning intentional time with my wife and daughter, and pausing before I responded when I felt tired or overwhelmed.

That change helped me become calmer, more present, and more responsible in my relationships. It reminded me that being proactive is not only about preventing problems at work. It is also about protecting peace at home. For me, overcoming that "cockroach" meant choosing response over reaction and making sure that my family receives the best of me, not the leftovers of my stress.
