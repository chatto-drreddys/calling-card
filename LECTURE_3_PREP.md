### Mini Case Study: Max & Max (16-min video)
- **Two protagonists:** Max (hunting dog) + Max (human employee)
- **Their boss:** Mr. Harold (manager of customer service at New World Imports)
- **The problem:** Mr. Harold emphasizes "Service. Service. Service." but then **forbids both Maxes from using their actual skills**
  - Max (dog) is trained to retrieve but told "stay on the lawn"
  - Max (human) solves a shipping crisis creatively but is reprimanded for deviating from policy
- **The failure:** When Harold finally gives them a real task (retrieve lost customers), both fail because they've learned to follow policy, not serve customers
- **Harold's blindness:** He's "perplexed by failure" but never realizes **his micromanagement is the problem**

### The 6 "Cancers" of Management (QUIZ-CRITICAL)

These are **destructive behaviors that prevent leaders from creating other leaders:**

1. **Cynicism** → Assumes bad motives; kills initiative
2. **Criticism** → Constant questioning without coaching; creates blame culture
3. **Comparing** → Measures people against rules, not mission; misaligns goals
4. **Competing** → Protects own turf; won't empower team
5. **Complaining** → Victimhood language; team internalizes helplessness
6. **Contending** → Unclear roles; eroded trust; "you're on your own" culture

**Mr. Harold's verdict (Class thumbs-down):** NOT a good leader. A "crooked leader" with "lots of gray areas needing improvement."

## The 8th Habit — core concepts to walk in knowing

### The one-line definition
> **"Find your voice, and inspire others to find theirs."**

The first 7 Habits make you **effective**. The 8th Habit takes you from effectiveness → **greatness** (contribution, significance, legacy). It's the bridge from *managing yourself* to *leading others*.

### Finding "your voice"
Your **voice** = your unique personal significance. It sits at the intersection of four things:
- **Talent** (your natural gifts/strengths)
- **Passion** (what energizes you)
- **Need** (what the world/your team needs)
- **Conscience** (the inner sense of what is right and worth doing)

→ When all four overlap, that's your voice. *(Tie-in: this is literally your Calling Card — Gift + Passion + Values + a need in the environment. For you: shaping environments, Enterprising + Investigative.)*

### The whole-person paradigm (high-yield for exam)
People are not things to be managed; they're whole people with four parts and four needs:

| Part of nature | Intelligence | Core need |
|---|---|---|
| **Body** | PQ (physical) | **To Live** |
| **Mind** | IQ (mental) | **To Learn** |
| **Heart** | EQ (emotional) | **To Love** |
| **Spirit** | SQ (spiritual) | **To Leave a Legacy** |

*(Notice it maps onto Habit 7's four renewal dimensions — physical, mental, social/emotional, spiritual. L may connect them.)*

### Leadership definition (likely tested)
> **Leadership is communicating people's worth and potential so clearly that they are inspired to see it in themselves.**

### Legacy & "Leadership in the Moment"
- **Legacy** = the "To Leave a Legacy" need. Greatness is measured by contribution that outlasts you, not just personal efficiency.
- **Leadership in the Moment** = real leadership shows up in small, unscripted, pressure-filled moments (the snack-attack / cockroach situations from last week) — *how you respond in the moment* is your leadership. Proactivity (Habit 1) + renewal (Habit 7) are what let you lead well in those moments.
