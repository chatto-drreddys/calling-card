# My Top 5 Values as a Leader

**Context:** Lead → Staff+ aspirant. Shaping Environments. 2 yrs client work. Learning AI/LLM/Systems Design.

**My Top 10 (locked):**
Mentoring, Learning, Direction, Integrity, Empowering, Teaming, Growth, Decisiveness, Truth, Stewardship

---

## Top 5 Values (Ranked)

### Rank 1: Integrity

**Why is this important to me?**
As a staff engineer navigating ambiguity and influence without authority (Larson, O'Reilly), integrity is my anchor—the only thing I fully control when technical solutions and organizational politics collide.

**How do I communicate this to others?**
Walk the talk: keep my word on technical commitments, admit failures openly, and make decisions transparent—even when it costs me short-term.

---

### Rank 2: Mentoring

**Why is this important to me?**
My gift is "Shaping Environments," and mentoring is how I scale that impact; I grow others so they can grow others, multiplying influence beyond my own technical output.

**How do I communicate this to others?**
Ask questions before giving answers; create psychological safety for junior engineers to think through hard problems; celebrate their wins publicly.

---

### Rank 3: Learning

**Why is this important to me?**
The staff engineer path requires constant growth in systems thinking, organizational dynamics, and emerging tech (Gen AI, LLMs); curiosity keeps me relevant and opens doors I can't yet see.

**How do I communicate this to others?**
Show up with "I don't know, let's figure it out" attitude; share what I'm learning openly; make learning a team activity, not gatekeeping.

---

### Rank 4: Direction

**Why is this important to me?**
As a fullstack engineer moving toward staff, I need a north star for where I'm heading (staff+ role, specific impact) so my daily choices compound toward that vision, not scatter.

**How do I communicate this to others?**
Articulate the "why" behind my decisions; help my team see how their work contributes to the larger direction; be clear about my north star and invite others to align.

---

### Rank 5: Empowering

**Why is this important to me?**
True leadership isn't about being the smartest person in the room; it's about unlocking the potential of others to solve problems I won't be present for—that's how I shape environments.

**How do I communicate this to others?**
Delegate ownership (not tasks); trust people with hard problems; give them air cover to experiment and fail; make it clear their voice matters in decisions.**
