## How to read the questions (from the sample quiz)

- Aptitude-style multiple choice. Some questions have **one** correct answer, some have **several**.
- Watch the stem: *"select all that apply"* / *"which of the following are…"* → multi-correct.
  *"best"* / *"most accurate"* → single answer.
- Multi-correct is where people lose marks — learn the **complete** list for every framework
  (4 dimensions, 5 steps, 6 steps, 3 characteristics, etc.).
- Definition-discrimination traps: paradigm vs principle, reactive vs proactive,
  react vs respond, victim vs victor, leading vs managing.

## MODULE 2 — Leadership in the Moment (Days 2–3)

### Darwin adage
> "It is not the strongest of the species that survive, nor the most intelligent, but the one most **responsive to change**."
Key words the exam may test: **responsive, adaptable, flexible, adjust**. Survival = adaptability, not strength/intelligence.

### Paradigm (high-yield definition)
- **Paradigm** = a set of **assumptions, concepts, values, and practices** that constitutes a way of viewing reality. It **determines behavior**; described as universal, timeless, inarguable, and it controls the consequences of our choices.
- **Paradigm can be POSITIVE or NEGATIVE.** A **principle is ALWAYS positive.** (Common trap.)
- (Stephen Covey) Paradigms — correct or incorrect — are the **source of our attitudes, behaviors, and ultimately our relationships** with others.
- Paradigms often form from subconscious/childhood experiences and upbringing.

### Victim vs Victor mentality
| Victim paradigm | Victor paradigm |
|---|---|
| Makes excuses, "woe is me" | "Despite circumstances, I'll rise to my greater self" |
| Blames others, points fingers | Embraces challenges as growth |
| Crushed by circumstances | "Circumstances are meant to create me, not crush me" |

### The Change Chart (sequence matters)
**Change** (circumstance / issue / shortcoming / any negative situation) → triggers your **Paradigm & Principles** → produces your **Behavior / Reaction**.

- **Negative path (Victim cycle):** negative paradigm → negative behavior → **fear / threat → fight-or-flight** (fight = aggressive, moody, blaming; flight = avoidance) → piles up **Resentment, Resistance, Revenge** (and Regret) → repeats as a cycle.
- **Break the cycle → Victor cycle:** adjust your paradigm/principles → seek advice (trusted peers, workmates, managers, or spiritual guidance) → **be proactive** → leads to **Choice, Opportunities, Creativity/Solutions** → ready for the next challenge without resentment.
- Quote (Beth Mende Connie): *"Life is a book and you are its author. You determine its plot and pace; only you turn its pages."*

### Habit 1 — Be Proactive
- Definition: **finding your mission in life and charting your own destiny** using the 4 human endowments: **Self-awareness, Creativity (imagination), Conscience, Will (willpower).**
- "Decide what you want, take responsibility for your decisions; face reality, accept what you cannot control, and choose a positive response."

**Reactive vs Proactive (memorize both columns):**
| Reactive | Proactive |
|---|---|
| Reacts | Responds |
| Blames; affected by physical environment | Takes responsibility; copes with & controls environment |
| Feels out of control | Chooses own behavior |
| Acts on **feelings** / conditions | Acts on **values** / conscious choice |
| Language: **"I can't, I must, if only"** | Language: **"I can, I will, I prefer"** |

**3 characteristics from the proactive video:**
1. Proactive people **don't blame**; reactive people blame circumstances, conditions, and conditioning.
2. Proactive behavior = product of **conscious choice based on values**; reactive = product of **conditions based on feelings**.
3. Proactive people are still influenced by external stimulus but their **response is a value-based choice**; reactive people build their lives around others' emotions.

**3 ways to be more proactive:** ① **Predict and prevent** (anticipate problems) ② **Plan** (daily agendas / to-do lists) ③ **Clear goals & vision** — set milestones, consult someone experienced, surround yourself with passionate people.

### MASTER checklist (handling adversity proactively)
**M**easure (how important is it, scale 1–5) → **A**ssess (your initial reaction) → **S**ee (the stimulus–response model) → **T**hink (a proactive response) → **E**ngage (use the chosen response **or exit**) → **R**eview (how proactive you were).
- It's OK to *react* internally; just **don't act on the reaction** — choose your response.

### Cockroach story (key distinction — high-yield)
- It's not the problem (cockroach) but your **inability to handle the disturbance** that disturbs you.
- **Reactions are instinctive → chaos. Responses are intellectual → control.**
- "More than the problem, the **reaction** to the problem matters." The women **reacted**; the waiter **responded**. → **Respond, don't react.**

### Habit 7 — Sharpen the Saw
- = **constantly renewing yourself in 4 basic dimensions**: **Physical, Social/Emotional, Mental, Spiritual.** (Memorize all 4.)
- Renewal must be **balanced** — neglecting one dimension negatively impacts the rest.
- "We are the **instruments of our own performance**" — investing in yourself is the single most powerful investment.

**Covey's 4-dimension challenge:**
| Dimension | Assume… | Now… |
|---|---|---|
| Body | you've had a heart attack | diet & exercise accordingly |
| Mind | the half-life of your profession is 2 years | prepare/keep learning accordingly |
| Heart | everything you say about others, they overhear | speak accordingly |
| Spirit | a quarterly one-on-one with your creator | live accordingly |

- **Productivity rule:** take a **5–10 minute break every hour**; don't work to exhaustion (dull-saw vs sharp-saw example — sharpening saves time and reduces errors).
- **4 ways to simplify life:** schedule downtime daily; plan to pay off credit; leave the TV off one night a week; don't be a slave to your phone (+ be patient with yourself).

### 5 Steps to Inner Renewal (memorize all 5)
**Remember** (capture a dream, make it real) → **Create** (make something from something simple) → **Absorb** (savor a gift that's yours — music, a flower) → **Want** (a want) → **Need** (deeper than a want; must be addressed at all costs).
- Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs was referenced here (want vs need).
